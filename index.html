Disablities Studies

Disablities studies is something that is extremely over looked, and there are many different views of this topic and many different arguments to be made. The main focus of our project was to look into how people with disablities are treated in the work place. We feel that they are not treated equal to everyone else in these spaces. We narrowed down and focused some of our research on autism. How this can improve is by giving these adults more opportunities out in the workforce, because right now there are little opportunities for disabled people to strive.

About

Our topic is on disabilities within workplaces. More specifically focused on autism. This is an important topic to talk about because it is very challenging for people with autism or any disability to find a comfortable workplace or even a job. We believe that it should not be a struggle for those that are diagnosed because most applicants that have autism are rejected right away. Another point to talk about is that only about 50% of the working population with disabilities are actually employed. To add to that people with disabilities receive a lower income rate because of their challenges. This issue should be more focused on since it involves discrimination. An example of this would be an experience that a classmate's cousin had. They were diagnosed with autism, however they are able to work and live independently. They applied to many jobs but were only accepted to do an interview for one of the many jobs they applied to. During the interview, the classmate’s cousin was asked multiple times if they were “sane enough” to work with other people and do the job properly. The cousin was very uncomfortable with the questions and side comments and decided that this workplace would not be a great place. As you can see, people with autism go through discrimination even during the interviews. The cousin being asked if they’re “sane enough” is disrespectful to them as humans since they are being looked at as people that can’t function in society. People with autism are human too and should be treated as individuals. It’s important to address this issue because it makes life easier for them and their families/caregivers. It also enhances our society by accepting differences and opens up new learning opportunities. A way to get involved with this issue is the project HOPE foundation. This organization help address discrimination towards people with autism and provides a community for them.

Employment for Individuals with Autism

Individuals with autism or any disability may experience finding a workplace that will hire them. This is because “only one in three (34.9%) individuals with disabilities are employed compared to 76% of their counterparts” (Bonaccio et al. 135). This statistic proves that individuals with autism do not have the same opportunities and are treated differently than people without disabilities. Our group believes that there is an urgent need for change in this topic so that all young adults can have a meaningful and respectable presence in the workplace of their choice. Hiring young people with autism and other disabilities creates a unique and diverse workplace. Many places of work “ continue to encounter ableism, social exclusion, inaccessible physical environments, lack of accommodations and modifications to existing facilities and practices, inadequate support and policies, intentional exclusion and segregations.

Work Place

There was this study that focused on how people with Parkinson's diseases are treated in the workplace. It mainly was about the duration of employment of people that were diagnosed with Parkinson’s. The study went around and interviewed people that were employed or unemployed. The researchers found out that only 15.3% of people with Parkinson’s disease were employed for more than 5 years. The researchers also found that people with Parkinson’s had a large amount of stress after leaving their job, they left because of severity of symptoms, lack of support in the workplace, and early retirement. This study showed that it is hard for people with Parkinson’s to be employed for more than 5 years.

Project Hope

Project Hope is an organization for autism awareness. It was founded in 1997 by two mothers who were looking for help for their two young sons. The organization focuses on four core services: education, therapy, adult services, and community engagement. The group is always holding events, fundraisers, and activities. To help with these, citizens are always welcome to volunteer and donate; it is a great way for citizens to become involved with this project.

This journal focuses mainly on what accommodations are made in the classroom to help provide individuals with the best education possible. The journal examines teacher’s perceptions of students with disabilities and most general educators were found to be positive when it came to dealing with desirability/effectiveness and reasonability/feasibility of making instructional adaptations for students with disabilities. On the other hand, research has also shown that when these students are included in general classroom education, teachers are less likely to “alter their traditional whole-group instructional strategies in favor of specific individualized adaptations.” This issue occurs because teachers are demonstrating lack of training and “limited school support as barriers to classroom teachers' being able to accommodate the individual needs of students in inclusive settings.” This is important to acknowledge because students with disabilities may feel left out of situations with classmates.
