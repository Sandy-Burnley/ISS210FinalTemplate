About

The legacy of colonialism has endured from its creation throughout all of history. The only way to combat colonialism is education and action. However, people seldom consider how these ideologies affect people’s lives and society over time. The colonization and assimilation of marginalized groups have significant negative effects on the marginalized societies in all aspects, including cultural, traditional, and religious beliefs. This issue is important to us as we believe that everyone deserves equal economic and social opportunities no matter their culture or beliefs. Our decision to broadcast this issue stems from the idea that our society and many others have been built off colonization with little consideration of how it affects colonized groups. It is important to educate ourselves of injustices and inequalities present within society to promote equity across all of society.

Shackled to the Past: The Causes and Consequences of Africa’s Slave Trade

Open this PDF in a new window


The African slave trade, starting in the mid-17th century, displaced millions of Africans from their homes and separated them from their families. Although the slave trade occurred over 200 years ago, the effects are still felt today. According to the article “Shackled to the Past: The Causes and Consequences of Africa’s Slave Trade”, if the slave trades had not occurred then the average income per capita of African countries would be between $2,679 and $5,158. Between 28 and 100% of the income gap between Africa and the developing world would not exist if the slave trades had not occurred.
