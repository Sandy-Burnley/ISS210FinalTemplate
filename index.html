How Global Warming and Climate Change Affect Our Farmers

Global warming and climate change are causing an increase in temperature and changing whether patterns. This is causing a change in how farmers have to grow their crops and manage their livestock.

Troy spoke about how the constant change of whether patterns make it hard to know when to start planting crops. For example, Troy expressed how we continue getting snow later and later into the year, making it hard to know when the right time to have workers start planting; if crops are planted while the whether is too cold, they will not be able to grow. How Climate Change is Shifting Whether Patterns and Harming Crops

This is the warmest period of time in the history of moden civilizaiton; furthermore, the human-caused climate change will continue for possibly a millenia. This human-caused climate change is causing changes in whether patterns, this can largely be seen in the raise in recent temperatures. Some results of this climate change on agriculture includes shifting precipitation patterns, more extreme wildfires, acceleration in the depletion of water suplies, and the increased spread of disease for crops and livestock. These will also have a secondary affect on the price of agricultural goods bought in stores, which causes a rise in inflation. Some crops productions that will be largely altered by climate change include the following: corn, soybeans, rice, sorghum, cotton, oats, and silage. https://www.ers.usda.gov/topics/natural-resources-environment/climate-change/

Harmful Agriculture Practices are Increasing Deforestation

Harmful agribusiness companies clear forests in order to create land to make marketable products such as beef and butter. Many companies have promised to stop ther harmful practices but lied as it would lower profits. As a result, the rate of deforestation has never been higher and agriculture businesses continue exploiting the forest area to grow their business. The Effect of Livestock on Climate Change

The way we raise livestock in the US contributes to the emissions of greenhouse gasses which can lead to climate change. According to Sentient Media Methane and Nitrous Oxide are released by the practice of rearing farm animals. These gasses contribute to at least 16.5% of greenhouse gas pollution in the world. Furthermore, animal agriculture is the driving factor of deforestation in the Brazilian Amazon.
