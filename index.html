Disablities Studies

Disablities studies is something that is extremely over looked, and there are many different views of this topic and many different arguments to be made. The main focus of our project was to look into how people with disablities are treated in the work place. We feel that they are not treated equal to everyone else in these spaces. We narrowed down and focused some of our research on autism. How this can improve is by giving these adults more opportunities out in the workforce, because right now there are little opportunities for disabled people to strive.

Employment for Individuals with Autism

Title of First Image

Individuals with autism or any disability may experience finding a workplace that will hire them. This is because “only one in three (34.9%) individuals with disabilities are employed compared to 76% of their counterparts” (Bonaccio et al. 135). This statistic proves that individuals with autism do not have the same opportunities and are treated differently than people without disabilities. Our group believes that there is an urgent need for change in this topic so that all young adults can have a meaningful and respectable presence in the workplace of their choice. Hiring young people with autism and other disabilities creates a unique and diverse workplace. Many places of work “ continue to encounter ableism, social exclusion, inaccessible physical environments, lack of accommodations and modifications to existing facilities and practices, inadequate support and policies, intentional exclusion and segregation.” (Lindsay et al. 6) This is the exact reason that workplaces should change their ways to be more inclusive to all individuals with disabilities. Bonaccio, Silvia, et al. “The Participation of People with Disabilities in the Workplace Across the Employment Cycle: Employer Concerns and Research Evidence.” Journal of Business & Psychology, vol. 35, no. 2, Apr. 2020, pp. 135–58. EBSCOhost, https://doi.org/10.1007/s10869-018-9602-5. Lindsay, Sally, et al. “Ableism and Workplace Discrimination Among Youth and Young Adults with Disabilities: A Systematic Review.” Journal of Occupational Rehabilitation, Aug. 2022. Work Place

There was this study that focused on how people with Parkinson's diseases are treated in the workplace. It mainly was about the duration of employment of people that were diagnosed with Parkinson’s. The study went around and interviewed people that were employed or unemployed. The researchers found out that only 15.3% of people with Parkinson’s disease were employed for more than 5 years. The researchers also found that people with Parkinson’s had a large amount of stress after leaving their job, they left because of severity of symptoms, lack of support in the workplace, and early retirement. This study showed that it is hard for people with Parkinson’s to be employed for more than 5 years.
